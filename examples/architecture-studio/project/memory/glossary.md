# Project Glossary

## Projects

- Project Atrium: office reception refresh using acoustic panels, stone-look surfaces, and warm task lighting.
- Project Garden Flat: residential kitchen and living-room update with durable cabinet fronts and family-safe surface choices.
- Project Lantern Row: townhouse entry and corridor lighting refresh, paired with a compact storage wall.

## Roles

- Lead Designer: owns concept direction and client-facing design decisions.
- Materials Lead: compares finishes, samples, durability, and maintenance.
- Lighting Consultant: checks color temperature, glare, dimming, and fixture placement.
- Site Coordinator: tracks access constraints without storing access secrets.
- Client Sponsor: privacy-safe alias for the client decision maker.

## Local Terms

- Sample ladder: a baseline, stretch, and fallback material option tested side by side.
- Quiet palette: low-contrast finishes chosen to reduce visual noise in small rooms.
- Morning mockup: a sample review performed in early daylight before final approval.
- Decision card: a short record of what changed, why, and what can be reused.
- Global vault: the client-neutral wiki for reusable studio lessons.
- Blocked field: a note that a sensitive source detail existed but was intentionally not stored.

## Do Not Store

- Address code: use a room or building type instead.
- Budget note: use broad bands only, such as "entry", "mid-range", or "premium".
- Supplier quote: summarize as a material category and price band.
- Private photo: describe the design constraint without storing the image.
